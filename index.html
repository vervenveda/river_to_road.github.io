The mission

Stewardship begins where we stand.

River-to-Road equips ordinary people to care for the places they use every day. The model is simple: gather safely, remove litter responsibly, protect wildlife, and leave each place better than we found it.

  • 01 Remove waste safely Use gloves, grabbers, visibility gear, and clearly marked bags.
  • 02 Protect water and wildlife Keep plastics, chemicals, and debris from entering rivers, streams, wetlands, and storm drains.
  • 03 Build lasting local pride Invite neighbors, youth, civic groups, artists, educators, and businesses to work side by side.

Every contribution directly supports hands-on community clean-up.

Community-wide invitation

Every last Saturday

Choose a safe public area in your own community and organize a responsible local clean-up.

◎WhereRoadways, ditches, rivers, streams, parks, trails, and shared public spaces.
◷WhenThe last Saturday of each month—or another safe day selected by your local group.
◇Safety firstFollow local laws, adult-supervise youth, remain visible, avoid traffic, and never handle hazardous waste.

A simple local model

Plan a clean-up in four careful steps.

Select a safe location

Choose a manageable public area. Check ownership, permissions, parking, traffic conditions, tides, weather, and disposal rules before inviting others.

Prepare people and supplies

Bring gloves, grabbers, bags, water, first-aid supplies, sunscreen, closed-toe shoes, reflective clothing, and a clear safety plan.

Sort and document responsibly

Separate recyclables when facilities permit. Photograph progress without exposing private information, and report large or hazardous items to local authorities.

Dispose, reflect, and return

Confirm that collected materials reach an approved disposal site. Thank volunteers, share the result, and note improvements for the next clean-up.
